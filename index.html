Any Pronouns

When someone says they use "any pronouns," it means they are comfortable with any set of pronouns being used to refer to them. This can include, but is not limited to, "he/him," "she/her," "they/them," and other pronoun sets. For example:

  • "He went to the store" or "She went to the store" or "They went to the store"
  • "I saw him yesterday" or "I saw her yesterday" or "I saw them yesterday"
  • "That's his book" or "That's her book" or "That's their book"

It's respectful to use a variety of pronouns when referring to someone who uses any pronouns. You may also ask if they have any preferences in certain contexts.
