She/They Pronouns

The pronoun set "She/They" indicates that a person is comfortable with both "she/her" and "they/them" pronouns. This means you can use either set when referring to them. For example:

  • "She went to the store" or "They went to the store"
  • "I saw her yesterday" or "I saw them yesterday"
  • "That's her book" or "That's their book"

It's respectful to alternate between these pronouns or ask the individual if they have a preference for one over the other in certain contexts.
